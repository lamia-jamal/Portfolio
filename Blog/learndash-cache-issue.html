How I Solved a LearnDash Course Completion Issue Caused by Caching

May 2026 · 4 min read

One of the most challenging LMS issues I worked on involved course completion tracking suddenly failing for users in a LearnDash-based learning platform.

At first, the issue appeared random. Learners were completing modules, but their progress was not updating correctly. Since the platform relied heavily on microlearning content and SCORM/xAPI tracking through GrassBlade xAPI Companion, this became a serious usability issue.

After investigating the affected courses, I discovered that the issue was related to caching at the hosting level. Cached data was preventing completion records from updating properly in real time.

Steps I Took

  • Reviewed the affected lesson and course setup
  • Verified GrassBlade xAPI activity tracking
  • Tested user progress updates
  • Compared working and non-working modules
  • Cleared the platform cache through WP Engine

Once the cache was cleared, course completion tracking immediately started functioning correctly again.

This experience taught me the importance of understanding how LMS systems, plugins, and hosting infrastructure interact together.
